About Me
Engineer
First.

I've spent years as a field engineer maintaining ATM networks and cash machine infrastructure — systems where downtime costs money by the minute and security isn't optional. That background shapes everything I build.

I understand how networks actually behave under pressure, what happens when hardware and software interact badly, and why fail-safe defaults matter more than clever features.

Now I'm applying that same thinking to software — building tools that are reliable, readable, and built to last. My projects lean toward security, automation, and tooling because that's where my real-world experience adds the most value.

I'm self-taught on the software side and proud of it. Every line of code I've written, I've had to earn by understanding it — not copying it.
